Case study

Coordinating dual shoots under pressure

As Project Manager at Beige Corporation, I was managing two shoots scheduled within the same week — one in Huntsville, Alabama, and another in Salt Lake City, Utah, for our CEO, Kawser. Both had tight timelines, shifting details, and contractor availability issues, making this a real test of coordination and resourcefulness.

Location crisis

Shoot 1: Huntsville

The Alabama shoot was first shared with me by Sales, but I was later told by our COO that the location was actually Huntsville, Texas — not Alabama. Trusting the update, I sourced and secured a contractor based in Texas.

Two days before the shoot, Client Success confirmed the real location was Alabama after all. With no viable leads left in our contractor database, I manually searched Google Maps and contacted local production studios directly — and partnered with the director of a local Huntsville, AL studio who agreed to work within our limited budget.

Short notice

Shoot 2: Salt Lake City

At the same time, I was managing a full-day shoot in Salt Lake City for our CEO — and none of our prioritized creative partners were available on short notice.

I expanded outreach beyond our database and connected with a skilled contractor who offered a 50% discounted rate. Contract execution, onboarding, and shoot coordination all happened within 2 hours. The shoot went so well that we inducted the contractor into Beige's CP Ambassador Program as a preferred vendor for future work.
