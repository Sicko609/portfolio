02 · GROUP THESIS, BRAC UNIVERSITY
In progress

Biofilm-Degrading Bacteriophages Against Waterborne Pathogens

Researcher · Data Analysis & Manuscript Writing

Investigating phages capable of degrading biofilms formed by pathogens isolated from urban lakes in Dhaka — work aimed at a phage-based alternative to antibiotics for waterborne disease control. My role spans the full research pipeline, from literature review and protocol design through lab work to data analysis and manuscript writing.

  • Conducted a structured literature review to ground the study's hypotheses and identify gaps in existing phage-therapy research on waterborne pathogens
  • Ran metagenomic profiling of water samples from urban Dhaka lakes to characterize the pathogen background before isolating candidate phages
  • Designed and refined lab protocols for phage isolation, propagation, and biofilm-formation assays, adapting standard methods to the specific sample conditions
  • Working with phages KpKal and KpKor, isolated against Klebsiella pneumoniae
  • Working with phages VcKal and JSF7, isolated against Vibrio cholerae
  • Running TTC (triphenyl tetrazolium chloride) staining assays and analyzing biofilm-reduction data in R
  • Drafting thesis sections, abstract, and conclusion with integrated citation work
